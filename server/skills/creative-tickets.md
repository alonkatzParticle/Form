## SECTION 1: BRAND IDENTITY & VOICE DNA

### 1.1 Brand Positioning
Particle for Men is a premium men's grooming and wellness brand. Core identity pillars:
- **"Aesthetic Science"** — The brand's proprietary framing. Not clinical pharma, not trendy beauty. Science that serves masculine aesthetics.
- **"For the Contemporary Gentleman"** — Not "guys" or "dudes." The customer is a man who has achieved things and wants his appearance to match his ambition.
- **Made in USA** — Quality, precision, trustworthiness. Used as a differentiator against generic/imported alternatives.
- **1,000,000+ customers** — The social proof anchor. Referenced constantly across scripts.
- **30-day money-back guarantee** — The risk-reversal that appears in virtually every CTA.
- **ParticleForMen.com** — The conversion destination. Always written as one word: particleformen.com

### 1.2 Voice Architecture
The Particle voice operates on a spectrum. Match voice register to the creative format:

**Register 1: Deadpan / Cynical (Pattern Interrupt ads, Animated Characters, Humor-driven)**
- Short sentences. Sentence fragments welcome.
- Self-aware about being an ad. ("Is this some kind of ad?" "Not until your shampoo fears me.")
- Pop culture references to masculinity. Grills, mortgages, taxes, gym, dating.
- Sarcasm that respects the viewer: "Your deodorant still leaves white marks on every shirt you own. Embarrassing."
- Never mean-spirited. The humor punches at the problem, not the man.

**Register 2: Direct / Authoritative (Science ads, Greenscreen, Expert-led)**
- Confident assertions backed by mechanism. "Body odor doesn't come from sweat. It comes from bacteria."
- Ingredient-to-outcome chains. "Capixyl targets hair loss. Redensyl reactivates follicles."
- Clinical vocabulary used sparingly for credibility, then immediately translated to plain English.
- Doctor/expert energy without being preachy.

**Register 3: Emotional / Intimate (Partner POV, Testimonial, Story arc)**
- First-person narration. Vulnerable opening, triumphant close.
- Specific sensory details: "His skin felt smoother," "She ran her fingers through his hair," "I caught a glimpse of him in the mirror."
- The emotional stakes are always relational: looking tired → feeling invisible → partner notices → confidence returns → intimacy reignites.

**Register 4: Native / UGC (TikTok-style, Creator-led, Caption-only)**
- Unpolished. Real. Like someone talking to their phone.
- "Look… most shampoos are just soap with a nice smell."
- Comments-style hooks: "Is there actually a shampoo that smells attractive on guys?"
- Zero brand-speak. If it sounds like marketing, rewrite it.

### 1.3 Voice Anti-Patterns — NEVER DO
- Never use "skincare routine" without immediately making it feel masculine ("ritual," "upgrade," "your daily edge")
- Never say "please try" or "we'd love for you to" — command, don't beg
- Never use emoji in scripts (only in TikTok-style comment overlays)
- Never say "anti-aging" without pairing it with a specific visible outcome
- Never reference "beauty" — use "grooming," "performance," "upgrade," "results"
- Never use generic superlatives ("amazing," "incredible," "wonderful") — be specific
- Never shame men for aging — reframe aging as something they can control
- Never use "unisex" or imply the product is for everyone — it's engineered for MEN
- Never write a script that sounds like it could be for a women's skincare brand
