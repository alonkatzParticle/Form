# BRAND OVERVIEW

## What Is Particle For Men?

Particle For Men is a science-led men's grooming brand built around a single premise: men were being ignored by the personal care industry. Founded after the brand's creators had their own bewildering experience standing in a drugstore aisle surrounded by pink, flower-scented products — none of which felt like they belonged — Particle was born to create formulations engineered specifically for men's skin and biology.
The brand's tagline: AESTHETIC SCIENCE FOR MEN. Every product is grounded in dermatological research, uses premium handpicked ingredients, and delivers multiple actions in a single product — eliminating the need for a complex multi-step regimen.

## Brand Mission & Origin

From the About Us page: "We were born out of a necessity to create a product that would better the lives of men. We saw that our demographic was being fiercely underrepresented, and we knew we needed to help men have choices in the grooming realm that was simply neglecting them."
Key founding story: Two men stood in a drugstore surrounded by beauty products — moisturizer, eye cream, anti-aging serum, dark spot treatment, night cream, day cream — and felt completely lost. They knew other men felt the same. Particle was the answer.

## Brand Pillars

- Science-first formulation — advanced dermatological research, molecular biology, cutting-edge actives
- Made for men — engineered for male skin, not repurposed from women's products
- Multi-action products — one product does what 5–6 used to do

The copy respects the customer's intelligence and speaks to his vanity without making him feel vain. # BRAND GUARDRAILS: DO NOT SAY / DO NOT DO

This section defines language, claims, tones, and framing that must be avoided in all Particle For Men ad creative, copy, scripts, and messaging. These guardrails protect brand integrity, legal compliance, and the trust Particle has built with over 1 million customers.

## 1. DO NOT MAKE — Unverified or Absolute Claims

**DO NOT SAY:** “Eliminates wrinkles” / “Removes eye bags completely” / “Cures hair loss”
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Diminishes the appearance of wrinkles” / “Visibly reduces eye bags” / “Fights hair loss and promotes growth”
WHY: Absolute claims (eliminates, cures, removes completely) are not substantiated by clinical trials and expose the brand to legal challenge. Particle uses qualified language: “reduces the appearance of,” “visibly diminishes,” “promotes,” “fights.”
**DO NOT SAY:** “Clinically proven to…” unless directly referencing a cited, published clinical study
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Formulated with dermatologically researched ingredients” / “Science-backed formula” / “Verified results”
**DO NOT SAY:** Specific guaranteed timeframes: “See results in 7 days guaranteed” or “Grow hair back in 2 weeks guaranteed”
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Most men see results within 2–3 weeks” / “Typically notice changes in approximately three weeks” / “Individual results may vary”
**DO NOT SAY:** “Works for everyone” or “100% effective”
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Safe and effective for all skin types, tones, and sensitivity levels” / “Results vary by individual”

## 2. ANTI-GRAY SERUM — Critical Language Rules

**DO NOT SAY:** “Hair dye” or “Colors your hair” or “Dyes gray hair” when referring to the Anti-Gray Serum
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Restores your natural pigment” / “Stimulates melanin production” / “Gradually reduces gray—without dye” / “Not a dye. A pigment restorer.”
**DO NOT SAY:** The serum “works on all hair colors”
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Ideal for light brown to black hair”—be explicit about the target hair color range
**DO NOT SAY:** “Permanently reverses gray hair”
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Gradually reduces gray with continued daily use”—requires ongoing use, results are not permanent if use is discontinued

## 3. HAIR REVIVAL KIT — Critical Language Rules

**DO NOT SAY:** “Works for bald men” or target completely bald men in creative
**SAY INSTEAD:** “For men experiencing early, mid, or late-stage hair thinning”. Target men with visible thinning—not total baldness
**DO NOT SAY:** “Regrows a full head of hair” or “Reverses baldness”
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Fights hair loss and promotes thicker, fuller hair” / “Helps hair grow back for men experiencing thinning”

## 4. TONE — What Particle Sounds Like vs. What It Does Not

**DO NOT USE:** Feminine-coded language—words like “glow,” “radiant,” “dewy,” “luminous,” “pampering,” “beauty ritual,” “skincare lover”
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Fresh,” “sharp,” “clean,” “defined,” “firm,” “powerful,” “confident,” “routine” (not “ritual”)
**DO NOT USE:** Bro-ish, locker-room, or hyper-masculine clichés—“buff,” “gains,” “alpha,” “sigma,” “beast mode,” “chad,” “grindset”
**SAY INSTEAD:** Confident, self-assured language. “Sharp,” “put-together,” “present,” “at your best.” Particle’s man is mature, successful, and discerning—not a gym stereotype.
**DO NOT USE:** Shame-based language or body-shaming angles—“You look old,” “Women hate a guy who looks tired,” “Your skin is embarrassing”
**SAY INSTEAD:** Aspiration-based copy. “Look like the man you feel like inside.” “Take a few years off your appearance.” Lead with the gain, not the shame.
**DO NOT USE:** Over-the-top urgency / false scarcity language—“LAST CHANCE!!!” “Buy NOW or miss out FOREVER.” Excessive caps, triple exclamation marks
**SAY INSTEAD:** Calm, confident urgency. “Selling out fast.” “Limited stock remaining.” “Free shipping on orders today.” Particle’s tone is premium—not discount-bin desperate.
**DO NOT USE:** Overly clinical / pharmaceutical tone—“Apply compound topically to affected dermis,” “Dermal matrix restructuring compound,” language that sounds like a prescription insert
**SAY INSTEAD:** Plain, confident science-speak: “Packed with Hyaluronic Acid, Coffee Seed Extract, and Shea Butter.” Name the ingredients—don’t over-technify the effect.

## 5. COMPETITOR & COMPARISON REFERENCES

**DO NOT:** Name specific competitor brands by name in ads (e.g., “Unlike Kiehl’s...” or “Better than Cetaphil”)
**SAY INSTEAD:** Generic category comparisons are fine: “Unlike typical moisturizers that do 1 thing, Particle does 6.” “Competitors use 1–2 active ingredients. Particle uses 12.”
**DO NOT:** Imply that drugstore or supermarket products are “for women”—the origin story frames it as products that weren’t made for men, not that they were feminine
**SAY INSTEAD:** “Nothing in that aisle was made with your skin in mind.” “The grooming industry ignored men for decades. We didn’t.”

## 6. FRAGRANCE COPY — Guardrails for Gravité & Varros

**DO NOT SAY:** Gravité and Varros are “the same” or “similar”—they are distinct and should never be conflated
**SAY INSTEAD:** Gravité = Bold, woody, seductive, powerful, 12+ hours, night-out presence. Varros = Fresh, elegant, light, versatile, daytime-forward, sophisticated.
**DO NOT:** Make explicit sexual claims—“Guaranteed to attract women,” “Makes women want to sleep with you”
**SAY INSTEAD:** Attraction and seduction can be implied—not stated explicitly. “She’ll ask what you’re wearing.” “Induces seduction.” “Leaves a resounding impression.” Use allure, not crudeness.

## 7. BEFORE/AFTER IMAGERY & TESTIMONIALS

**DO NOT:** Imply before/after results are guaranteed or typical without appropriate disclaimers
**ALWAYS INCLUDE:** “Before/after images shared by customers who have made a purchase. Results have not been independently verified. Individual results will vary.”
**DO NOT:** Fabricate or embellish customer quotes. Testimonials must reflect real customer experiences
**INSTEAD:** Use real quotes from Stamped.io reviews on site. Always include: name initial, age, city (format: “Andy N. | 51, Tucson, AZ”). This format is the Particle standard.
